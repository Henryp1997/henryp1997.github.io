Master's Project - A Dynamic Light Scattering Study
◦ I completed my Master's degree during the 2020-2021 academic year, right in the middle of the coronavirus pandemic. Therefore, due to our restricted access to the labs of Leeds University, my Master's project was very data-focused.
◦ Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS) is a technique used to probe the size, structure and dynamics of molecules and particles, commonly used to analyse the dynamics and motions of different solutions.
◦ My mission was to describe the relaxations of the dynamics of hydrogel samples, attempting to find a suitable model to describe the motions/relaxations of the sample over short, intermediate and long timescales.
◦ The hydrogel sample provides non-trivial results due to the crosslinks developed at certain critical temperatures and concentrations, leading to interesting and not very well agreed upon dynamics (particularly in the intermediate timescales).
